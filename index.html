Tidiane Guindo

I am an economist working on applied microeconometrics and causal inference, with a focus on innovation economics and industrial policy. I use administrative microdata and quasi-experimental methods to study policy design, targeting, and interactions affecting firm behavior and performance.

I am a Graduate Research Fellow at Andalas University, Padang, Indonesia, completing a Master of Economics in Public Policy (expected June 2026), under Profs. Syafruddin Karimi and Hefrizal Handra. I hold a Master of Innovation Management (Cum Laude, 4.0 GPA) from Sumbawa University of Technology.

Research

Work in Progress

  • Causal Identification of R&D Policy Effectiveness using Regression Discontinuity Designs (ASEAN)
    Estimates the causal impact of R&D expenditure thresholds and public funding on firm-level innovation, productivity, and knowledge spillovers across ASEAN economies, using sharp, fuzzy, and geographic regression discontinuity designs.
  • Firm Exposure and Policy Transmission in ASEAN Economies
    Studies heterogeneous firm responses to macroeconomic policy shocks using panel data methods, analyzing how firm characteristics mediate the transmission of policy to investment and productivity outcomes.
