Applied Microeconomics · Innovation · Causal Inference

Tidiane Guindo

I am an economist working on applied microeconometrics and causal inference, with a focus on innovation economics and industrial policy. I use administrative microdata and quasi-experimental methods to study policy design, targeting, and interactions affecting firm behavior and performance.

I am a Graduate Research Fellow at Andalas University (Padang, Indonesia), where I am completing a Master of Economics in Public Policy (expected June 2026), and hold a Master of Innovation Management (Cum Laude, 4.0 GPA) from Sumbawa University of Technology.

Research

Causal Identification of R&D Policy Effectiveness using Regression Discontinuity Designs (ASEAN) Work in Progress

Quasi-experimental methods · Sharp, Fuzzy, and Geographic RDD

Estimates the causal impact of R&D expenditure thresholds and public funding on firm-level innovation, productivity, and knowledge spillovers across ASEAN economies, using sharp, fuzzy, and geographic regression discontinuity designs.

Firm Exposure and Policy Transmission in ASEAN Economies Work in Progress

Panel data methods · Heterogeneous firm responses

Studies heterogeneous firm responses to macroeconomic policy using panel data methods, analyzing how different firm characteristics mediate the transmission of policy shocks to investment and productivity outcomes.

Who Absorbs External Shocks? Foreign Exchange Intervention, Monetary Policy, and Stabilization Trade-offs: A DSGE Study of ASEAN Economies

SN Business & Economics, 6, 52 (Springer Nature), 2026

Analyzes stabilization trade-offs between foreign exchange intervention and monetary policy in ASEAN economies using a Dynamic Stochastic General Equilibrium framework, examining which institutions absorb external shocks and at what cost.

ICT Adoption, Innovation, and Labor Productivity in ASEAN

With Harjito · Wab Academia Journal of Social Sciences, 4(2), 28–54, 2025

Examines the joint effects of ICT adoption and innovation on labor productivity across ASEAN economies using panel data analysis, identifying complementarities and country-level heterogeneity. Presented at the 9th SANEM Annual Economists' Conference (Dhaka, Bangladesh, April 2026).
